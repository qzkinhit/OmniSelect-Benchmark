## 可执行计划
### tabular — INCLUDE-NOW
- 池: Quality-variance pool built from small OpenML-CC18 / TabZilla tasks (e.g. credit-g, phoneme, electricity subsampled to <=1000 rows, <=100 features). High-quality = original rows; low-quality = injected label-flips + feature corruption. Held-out test = clean test splits of the same tasks, optionally extended to a handful more CC18 small tasks for cross-dataset rank.
- 模型: TabPFN v2 classifier (Prior-Labs/TabPFN-v2-clf, ~7M params). Selection = choosing the in-context support set under a fixed budget of k rows; pure forward-pass inference on M2 CPU/MPS, no training. Ablation: XGBoost / small MLP trained on the same selected subset to show effect is not TabPFN-specific.
- 评测: Held-out test splits of the OpenML-CC18 / TabZilla source tasks. Primary metric ROC AUC (OvR for multiclass), accuracy secondary; report mean and average rank across datasets over 3-5 seeds. Baselines: random, k-means coreset (TDColER convention), Tab-AICL (TabPFN-Coreset/Margin/Hybrid), full-pool.
- 防质疑: MODERATE-to-STRONG precedent for selecting/distilling data specifically for the tabular FM: ICD-TabPFN (NeurIPS TRL 2024), TDColER/TDBench (TMLR 2025), Tab-AICL (2026). A reviewer cannot call 'selection for the tabular FM' ad hoc. Critical framing discipline: for TabPFN, selection = in-context support-set selection (not gradient training), so the paper must say 'context-set selection' (or fine-tuning), not 'train the model on the selection', to stay faithful.
- M2工作量: LOW. Pure forward-pass inference, ~1.3s per dataset on CPU; <=1000 rows x <=100 features is comfortably feasible. No GPU, no training loop. Main cost is wiring the noise-injection pipeline and the 4-5 baseline selectors plus multi-seed sweeps. Roughly 3-5 days of engineering.

### timeseries — INCLUDE-RISKY
- 池: High-quality real corpus = LSF datasets (ETTh1/ETTh2/ETTm1/ETTm2, Weather, Electricity, Exchange) plus a Monash archive subset. Low-quality = injected corrupted/missing/low-SNR series and KernelSynth-style synthetic negatives, forming the quality-variance pool. Held-out = LSF test split + a Monash zero-shot subset (optionally a GIFT-Eval subset).
- 模型: Primary: small PatchTST-small (channel-independent, hundreds-of-K to a few-M params) trained from scratch on the selected subset under a fixed series/token budget; DLinear as the cheap baseline model. Optional transfer check: fine-tune Chronos-Bolt-Tiny (~9M) to show selection transfers to an actual TSFM.
- 评测: Held-out LSF test + Monash zero-shot subset. Primary metric MASE (scale-free, comparable across datasets); MAE/MSE for the deterministic PatchTST/DLinear; WQL/CRPS only if reporting probabilistic Chronos-Bolt. Compare selection vs random and full-pool at equal budget across multiple seeds; aggregate by geometric mean of relative scores (Chronos/Moirai convention).
- 防质疑: MODERATE-to-STRONG precedent that the TS community curates/filters/mixes pretraining data for its OWN FMs: Chronos TSMixup/KernelSynth, Moirai/LOTSA filtering, Time-MoE cleaning, and the 2025 'Data Mixture Search' paper. Weakness: most TS work frames this as augmentation/mixture/cleaning rather than fixed-budget coreset selection, and canonical TSFMs train on billions of points, so the from-scratch small-model-on-M2 setup must be argued explicitly as a controlled proxy, not a SOTA claim.
- M2工作量: MEDIUM. PatchTST-small/DLinear train end-to-end on M2 in a fixed budget, fully feasible. Costs: building the noise/low-SNR injection + KernelSynth negatives, the train-from-scratch loop with budget control, MASE plumbing, and optional Chronos-Bolt-Tiny fine-tune. Roughly 1-1.5 weeks; the framing argument (proxy not SOTA) is more work to defend than the compute.

### vision (image classification) — INCLUDE-NOW
- 池: CIFAR-100 train set with injected symmetric label noise / corrupted images to create the quality-variance pool (matches the ICLR 2025 CLIP-powered selection protocol and DeepCore conventions). Held-out = clean CIFAR-100 test set. Optional secondary: CIFAR-10 / Tiny-ImageNet for a robustness check.
- 模型: Two-tier. Cheapest defensible: frozen DINOv2 ViT-S/14 (or CLIP ViT-B/32) features extracted once, then a linear probe trained on the selected subset (seconds). Headline: ResNet-18 (~11M) trained from scratch on the selected subset via PyTorch MPS. Selection scorer uses the frozen embedding + a cheap scorer at fixed budget fractions (10-30% as in DeepCore).
- 评测: Clean CIFAR-100 held-out test, top-1 accuracy (linear-probe top-1 if frozen encoder), averaged over 3-5 seeds, with random-selection and full-data baselines, plus DeepCore-lineage baselines (forgetting/EL2N/CCS, GM-Matching).
- 防质疑: STRONG. Richest, most-cited data-selection precedent of any modality: DataComp (NeurIPS 2023) and DeepCore are whole benchmarks for selecting data for vision FMs/classifiers; the exact quality-variance-under-noise framing is mirrored by the CLIP-Powered Robust Selection paper (ICLR 2025) and GM-Matching (ICML 2025). No reviewer can call this ad hoc.
- M2工作量: LOW-to-MEDIUM. Linear-probe-on-frozen-features variant is trivial (seconds to train). ResNet-18 from scratch on CIFAR is ~10-14 s/epoch on M2 MPS, full runs in minutes-to-tens-of-minutes; multi-seed x multi-fraction x multi-baseline sweeps are the only real cost. All components torchvision-native and non-gated. Roughly 4-6 days.

### cross-modal unified model (truly modality-agnostic) — DEFER-APPENDIX
- 池: No new pool needed for the appendix: reuse the per-modality pools above. The appendix demonstrates that ONE modality-agnostic selection scorer/criterion produces consistent gains across text, tabular, timeseries, and vision pools, rather than training a single cross-modal FM.
- 模型: No unified foundation model is trained. The appendix shows the selection method (the scorer) transfers across modalities by applying the identical selection rule to each modality's own M2-feasible model (TabPFN, PatchTST-small, ResNet-18/linear-probe, plus the done text LM).
- 评测: Per-modality metrics already defined (ROC AUC, MASE, top-1 accuracy), reported as a single cross-modality consistency table (does the same selection criterion win in every modality at equal budget). No new benchmark.
- 防质疑: Training a genuinely unified cross-modal FM on M2 is not feasible and would draw a strong scale/SOTA challenge, so it must NOT be a headline claim. As an appendix it is defensible: the contribution is a modality-agnostic selection criterion that empirically transfers, evidenced by reusing existing per-modality results, with no new compute risk.
- M2工作量: LOW (as scoped). No new training; just re-running the same scorer across modalities and assembling the consistency table. The risk is purely framing: keep it as 'criterion transfers', never 'we trained a unified model'.

## 顺序与 go/no-go
Do the text core first (already done: language/math/code) and freeze its protocol as the template (quality-variance pool, equal-budget, multi-seed, random/coreset/full baselines). Then add modalities one-by-one, each behind a go/no-go gate so the paper degrades gracefully if a modality fails.

Order and gates:
1) TEXT CORE (done): lock the selection criterion, the equal-budget protocol, and the baseline set. GATE: text shows a clear, statistically meaningful win over random at equal budget. This is the spine; nothing proceeds without it.

2) VISION (add second, not timeseries). Rationale: strongest precedent (DataComp/DeepCore/ICLR-2025 CLIP-powered) and lowest framing risk, so it is the safest second modality to prove cross-modality generality. Start with the linear-probe-on-frozen-DINOv2 variant (trivial M2 cost) to get a fast signal, then add ResNet-18-from-scratch for the headline. GATE: selection beats random at equal budget on clean CIFAR-100 test across 3-5 seeds in at least the linear-probe variant. If yes, vision is a main-paper modality. If no, demote to ablation and stop investing.

3) TABULAR (add third). Low compute, genuine FM-selection precedent. Enforce the framing discipline up front (context-set selection, not gradient training). GATE: selection beats random AND is competitive-or-better than k-means coreset / Tab-AICL at fixed k, on ROC AUC mean+rank across the held-out tasks, multi-seed. Include the XGBoost ablation so the result is not TabPFN-specific. If the win is fragile, keep tabular but clearly scope it as context-set selection only.

4) TIMESERIES (add last, highest risk). Only after text+vision+tabular each clear their gates, so the paper already stands on three modalities. GATE (stricter, two-part): (a) train-from-scratch PatchTST-small on the selected subset beats random and approaches full-pool at equal budget on MASE across LSF + Monash zero-slot subset, multi-seed; AND (b) the proxy framing is written as a controlled study, not SOTA. If (a) fails or is noisy, DEFER timeseries to the appendix rather than dropping it (the curation precedents still support an appendix demonstration). The optional Chronos-Bolt-Tiny fine-tune is a stretch goal only if time remains.

5) CROSS-MODAL UNIFIED: appendix only, assembled at the end from existing per-modality results as a consistency table. Never a gate, never a headline.

Scope-creep discipline: each modality is added only after the previous one passes its gate; a failed gate demotes (to ablation/appendix) rather than triggering more experiments; no new pools or models are introduced beyond those listed.

## 诚实风险
Timeseries is the riskiest and least likely to show a clean selection benefit. Three reasons: (1) The canonical TSFMs train on billions of points; a from-scratch PatchTST-small/DLinear on M2 is a proxy, and a reviewer can argue that selection effects observed on a tiny model do not transfer to real TSFMs, so the SOTA-vs-proxy framing is the main exposure. (2) The TS literature frames data work as augmentation / mixture-search / cleaning (Chronos TSMixup-KernelSynth, Moirai-LOTSA, Time-MoE, Data-Mixture-Search 2025), NOT fixed-budget coreset selection, so the precedent supports 'curation matters' but only loosely supports 'budgeted subset selection', inviting a 'wrong-framing' challenge. (3) Forecasting performance on LSF/Monash is dominated by recent windows and seasonality; pruning whole series can hurt coverage, so the equal-budget selection win over random may be small or seed-sensitive, which is exactly why it sits behind the strictest gate and falls back to the appendix.

Secondary risk: tabular's challenge is narrower but real, the 'selection = in-context support set, not training' point. If the paper sloppily says 'we train TabPFN on the selection', a reviewer will flag it; mitigated entirely by correct framing (context-set selection / fine-tuning).

Vision and text are the safe anchors: vision has the strongest, most-cited precedent and cheapest compute, so the paper should lean on text+vision (+tabular) as the defensible core and treat timeseries as upside, not load-bearing."
